# financebench — Tavily competence and gaps

## Headline

Tavily advanced's biggest gap is retrieval coverage on historical SEC filings: 21 of its losses carry a `retrieval_gap` failure mode, meaning the system returned empty answers on questions that both Exa and even Tavily advanced itself could resolve when the right document was found. Its clearest area of competence is multi-step calculation and period disambiguation on 10-K and 8-K filings, where Tavily advanced beats Exa on at least 10 questions where competitors either returned empty answers or cited the wrong figure.

## Where Tavily wins

- **Multi-step ratio calculations from primary filings.** Tavily advanced correctly computed AES's FY2022 ROA of -0.02 (Q17) and Amazon's FY2017 DPO of 93.86 (Q18) using four-component formulas, while Exa returned empty answers on both. On the Amazon DPO, both competitors failed entirely; Tavily advanced was the sole correct provider.

- **Period disambiguation on comparative questions.** Tavily advanced correctly identified Boeing's improving gross margin (4.8% to 5.3%, FY2021-FY2022, Q62) and Netflix's FY2017 current liabilities of $5,466M (Q113) while Exa cited the wrong direction (declining margin) or wrong figure ($5,122.945M). These wins trace to Tavily advanced's `correct_period_disambiguation` success factor appearing twice in the wins set.

- **Resolving "none" answers from ambiguous debt registry questions.** On Q38, Tavily advanced correctly concluded American Express had zero registered exchange-traded debt securities in 2022, while Exa incorrectly listed a specific security (3.433% Notes due 2032). This `ambiguity_resolved` pattern matters for compliance-adjacent financial queries where a false positive is worse than a blank.

- **Segment-level attribution against analyst-style questions.** Tavily advanced correctly identified 3M's Consumer segment as the FY2022 organic growth drag at -0.9% (Q4) while Exa misidentified Safety and Industrial. Tavily advanced also correctly enumerated J&J's FY2022 gross margin drivers (COVID vaccine exit costs, currency, commodity inflation) where Exa dismissed the metric entirely as non-useful (Q86).

- **Outperformance on 8-K point-in-time data.** On Q127, Tavily advanced correctly aggregated PepsiCo's total revolving credit capacity at $8.4B as of May 26, 2023 by combining both the new five-year and the 364-day facilities; Exa and basic both returned $4.2B, missing the second facility. On Q126, Tavily advanced and Exa both retrieved the $400M increase correctly, but Tavily basic failed entirely on both 8-K questions.

## Where Tavily falls short

- **Retrieval gaps on historical filings are the dominant failure mode (21 losses).** The pattern is consistent: Tavily basic returns an empty answer, and Tavily advanced often returns one too, on questions requiring a specific line item from a pre-2021 SEC filing. Examples include Q10 (Adobe FY2015 operating cash flow ratio, Exa solved it), Q6 (3M Q2 2023 registered debt securities, Exa solved it), Q43 (American Express FY2022 largest balance sheet liability, Exa solved it), Q83 (General Mills FY2020 FCF, Exa solved it), and Q122 (PepsiCo FY2022 restructuring costs, Exa solved it). The source type is consistently a 10-K income statement, balance sheet, or cash flow statement line item. The fix is not a prompt change — the index either has the document or it does not. A dedicated financial statements corpus covering SEC EDGAR filings from 2015-2023 for the FinanceBench company set is the concrete gap.

- **Wrong line item or wrong fiscal year on direct-lookup questions (10 calculation errors).** On Q0, Tavily advanced extracted $1,603M for 3M's FY2018 capex instead of $1,577M. On Q116, both Tavily variants extracted Nike's inventory turnover as 3.59 from a secondary aggregator rather than computing 3.46 from primary statement inputs (COGS $24,576M / average inventory $7,110.5M). On Q74, Tavily basic pulled Costco FY2022 total assets ($64,166M) instead of FY2021 ($59,268M). These failures share a root cause: retrieval surfaces a secondary financial aggregator (Yahoo Finance, Macrotrends) with slightly different figures rather than the primary 10-K filing, and the model accepts that figure without cross-checking. A re-ranking rule that penalizes non-SEC aggregator sources for calculation queries would close several of these.

- **Wrong period citations on quarterly filings (4 losses).** On Q5, Tavily basic cited December 2023 quick ratio (0.6x) instead of Q2 FY2023 (0.96). On Q51, Tavily basic dated Best Buy's Current Health acquisition to FY2021 instead of FY2022. The 10-Q corpus coverage appears thin relative to 10-K coverage. These are not reasoning failures — the model knew the formula and the company — it retrieved the wrong period's document.

- **Guidance and qualitative attribution failures on earnings releases.** On Q128, Tavily advanced cited operational specifics (elasticity, supply chain) as the reason PepsiCo raised FY2023 guidance, when the benchmark answer is the higher-level narrative: "strong start to FY2023." On Q3, Tavily basic could not retrieve any operating margin drivers for 3M FY2022 (Tavily advanced succeeded). Earnings transcript and earnings release documents are the missing source type here; these answers live in management commentary, not in the financial statements tables.

- **Pfizer is a near-total retrieval failure across all providers (5/5 questions wrong, Q130, Q132, Q133, Q135, and one more).** Tavily advanced returned empty answers or wrong company names on all Pfizer questions. The specific 10-K cited in the benchmark (the FY2021 filing referencing Trillium, Array, and Therachon acquisitions, and the Upjohn spinoff) is evidently not indexed. This is a single-company indexing gap but it accounts for a disproportionate share of Health Care losses.

- **Complex working capital and fintech-specific metric definitions (9 wrong_metric_definition losses).** On Q118, Tavily advanced dismissed working capital as irrelevant for PayPal (a fintech) instead of computing the operating current assets minus operating current liabilities approach the benchmark expects ($1.6B). On Q120, Tavily advanced reported "North America and International" for PepsiCo's geographic operations instead of listing all seven specific regions. These are cases where the model's prior about the appropriate metric overrides what the question is actually asking. A system prompt instruction to compute the metric as literally defined in the question, without substituting a "more appropriate" alternative, would address this pattern.

## Cross-cutting observations

- **Tavily basic is nearly non-functional on this benchmark at 12.9% accuracy.** The gap between basic (12.9%) and advanced (57.1%) is 44.2pp — the largest separation among any provider pair. Basic returns empty answers on the vast majority of calculation and multi-step extraction questions. Every question type where basic fails is one where the answer requires locating a specific number from a dated SEC filing. Basic should not be positioned for any financial statement analysis use case without the same document corpus as advanced.

- **10-Q coverage is materially weaker than 10-K coverage for Tavily advanced.** On 10-K questions, Tavily advanced hits 59.2% (29/49 correct). On 10-Q questions it drops to 28.6% (2/7 correct), a 30.6pp gap. Exa holds 57.1% on 10-Qs. The quarterly filing cadence — with period-specific balance sheets that change every quarter — is where Tavily advanced most frequently cites the wrong period or returns empty. This is consistent with a corpus that skews toward annual filing indexing.

- **Earnings release documents (doc_type "Earnings") are a weak spot.** Tavily advanced scores 42.9% (3/7) on Earnings questions versus 85.7% (6/7) on 8-K questions. Both document types are relatively recent and event-driven. The 8-K outperformance suggests the gap is specific to the non-GAAP reconciliation tables and management commentary sections found in earnings releases, not in SEC filing retrieval generally.

- **Joint hard questions concentrate on specific ratio calculation errors that all providers share.** The `joint_hards` set includes AMD quick ratio (all providers cite 1.77 vs. expected 1.57, Q31), Corning working capital (Exa returns $5.6B vs. expected $831M, Q73), and Netflix FY2015 unadjusted EBITDA margin (Exa calculates 28.8% by including streaming content amortization, Q112). These are not retrieval failures — data is found but the formula application is wrong, often because the benchmark uses a non-standard definition that excludes certain line items. The diagnosis for Q31 is that all three providers appear to pull a quick ratio from a secondary aggregator using a different formula than the benchmark's specific definition (which includes receivables from related parties).

## What to do next

- **Build and index a FinanceBench-specific SEC EDGAR corpus covering all 28 companies in this benchmark for 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, and earnings releases from 2015-2024.** The 21 `retrieval_gap` losses are not solvable with prompt engineering — the primary filing pages are either not indexed or ranked too low. Prioritize Pfizer (0 of 5 questions correct, all retrieval failures), 3M 10-Qs, and pre-2020 Adobe and Nike annual filings. This is the highest-leverage single action.

- **Add a source-type re-ranking rule that downgrades financial aggregator pages (Macrotrends, Yahoo Finance, Wisesheets) in favor of SEC EDGAR URLs when the query contains a specific fiscal year and a primary statement keyword (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement).** This directly addresses the 10 `calculation_error` losses where the wrong figure came from an aggregator with different rounding or a misaligned fiscal year definition (Q0, Q74, Q116).

- **Add an explicit system prompt instruction: "When a question defines a specific formula, apply exactly that formula using primary statement line items. Do not substitute an alternative metric, even if you believe a different metric is more standard."** This targets the `wrong_metric_definition` pattern (9 losses) including Q118 (PayPal working capital dismissed as irrelevant), Q120 (PepsiCo geography answered at segment level instead of specific geography list), and Q128 (guidance rationale answered with operational specifics instead of stated management reasoning).

- **Route all questions specifying a quarter (Q1, Q2, Q3, or a month-end date) to a retrieval path that filters to 10-Q filings for that specific quarter, not the most recent 10-K.** Four `wrong_period` losses (Q5, Q6, Q51) share the pattern that Tavily retrieved the closest available filing rather than the period-specific one. A metadata filter on filing date range tied to the quarter mentioned in the query would eliminate these.

- **For multi-step ratio calculations where the question provides the formula explicitly, add a chain-of-thought extraction step that first identifies each variable from the source document before computing the ratio.** The wins on Q17 (AES ROA) and Q18 (Amazon DPO) show Tavily advanced can do this correctly. The losses on Q10 (Adobe OCF ratio, empty answer) and Q116 (Nike inventory turnover, wrong aggregator value) show it is inconsistent. A structured extraction prompt — list each variable, cite the line item and page, then calculate — would reduce both empty-answer and wrong-source failures on this question type.
